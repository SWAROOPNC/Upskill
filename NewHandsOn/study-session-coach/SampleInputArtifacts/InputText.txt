Prompt engineering is the practice of designing and refining inputs (prompts) to get the best possible outputs from AI models like ChatGPT or other large language models.

---

What it really means

At its core, prompt engineering is about communicating clearly with AI. Since models don’t “think” like humans, the way you phrase a request strongly affects the result.

A simple prompt:

“Explain gravity.”

A better-engineered prompt:

“Explain gravity in simple terms for a 10-year-old, using examples from everyday life.”

Same topic—very different output quality.

---

Key techniques

1. Be specific

Vague prompts lead to vague answers. Clear prompts lead to useful answers.

Example:
“Write something about business”
vs
“Write a 200-word explanation of how startups raise funding in early stages”

---

2. Set context or role

Tell the AI who it should act like.

Examples:
“Act as a marketing expert…”
“Explain like a physics professor…”
“Respond as a beginner-friendly tutor…”

---

3. Define format

You can control structure.

Examples:
“Give bullet points”
“Write a table”
“Step-by-step instructions”

---

4. Provide examples (few-shot prompting)

Show the AI what you want.

Example:
Input: Apple → Fruit
Input: Carrot → Vegetable
Input: Salmon → ?

This helps guide pattern recognition.

---

5. Break complex tasks into steps

Instead of:
“Write a business plan”

Use:

1. List business ideas
2. Pick the best one and justify
3. Create a business plan outline

---

6. Use constraints

Limits improve quality.

Examples:
“In under 100 words”
“Use simple language”
“Avoid technical jargon”

---

Types of prompts

Zero-shot: No examples
Few-shot: With examples
Chain-of-thought: Ask AI to explain reasoning step by step
Instruction-based: Direct commands like “Summarize” or “Translate”

---

Why it matters

Good prompt engineering helps you:
Get more accurate answers
Save time with fewer revisions
Unlock advanced use cases like coding, research, and automation

---

Simple formula

A strong prompt often looks like:

Role + Task + Context + Format + Constraints

Example:

“Act as a career coach. Give me 5 resume tips for a recent graduate applying to tech jobs. Use bullet points and keep it under 150 words.”
